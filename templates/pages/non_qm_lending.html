Non QM Loans

A Non QM Loan is any loan that does not meet the Qualified Mortgage requirements set forth by the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau.

The Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act imposed an obligation on lenders to make a good-faith effort to determine that the applicants have the ability to repay the mortgage. This is known as the ability-to-repay (ATR) rule. The Act also mandates that QM loans cannot have risky loan features like negative amortization, interest-only, balloon payments, terms beyond 30 years or excessive points and fees.

A Qualified Mortgage loan must satisy at least one of the following:

  • Debt to Income ratio of 43% or less
  • Loan would be eligible through the FHA, VA, USDA, or government sponsored enterprise programs
  • Meet USDA monthly income caps
  • Loan was originated by insured depositories with total assets less than $10 billion and must be held in portfolio for at least three years.

Any loan that does meet the above standards is categorized as a Non QM Loan.
